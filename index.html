Position

Joseph Gentleman is a Lynn native who has over 20 years of experience working at the Southern Essex District Registry of Deeds.  Joseph had the pleasure of working in the Salem office under longtime Register of Deeds John O'Brien who ran the best Registry of Deeds in Massachusetts. Joseph is a U.S. Navy Veteran, former Secretary-Treasurer for the IAM Local Union S-25 Ship Builders in Boston and has held many leadership roles in the community, including running many foundations.  Joseph is a member of the Lions Clubs International organization which donates humanitarian service locally and around the globe. Joseph is married to Jeannine Gentleman (Langlais), also a native of Lynn, MA.  Joseph and Jeannine are both graduates of Lynn Classical High School and have lived in Peabody for the past 28 years. Joseph is the father of the late Tim Gentleman and father-in-law of the late Laura Gentleman and has five grandchildren.

Platform

Platform? Joseph is running for the office of Southern Essex District Register of Deeds to help homeowners protect their homes and to fight fraud by promoting the Homestead Act and the Property Fraud Watch program.  Joseph has a working knowledge of the Registered Land department and will continue to follow the Massachusetts Deed Indexing Standards to ensure that property owners are able to easily find documents relevant to their search.  He aims to enhance customer service and to improve registry services to make them more accessible and efficient for the community. Joseph's goal is to bring his experience and dedication to the role of Register of Deeds, ensuring the registry serves the public efficiently and effectively.  EXPERIENCE MATTERS!

Letters of Appreciation working at the Registry of Deeds:
Joseph received numerous letters for his excellent customer service and will continue to focus on service once elected. Letters of Appreciation

Why have you decided to run for Register of Deeds?
To help fight fraud and protect home ownership and enforce the Indexing&Standards required in all Massachusetts Registries and continue to be one of the best run Registries with excellent customer service.
As the current President of the Lynn Lions Club and Director at Camp Lions, Joe has demonstrated strong leadership in community service. His past roles include President of the Visual Aids Foundation Inc. and Vice President of Eye Mobile Inc., emphasizing his dedication to health initiatives and community well-being.

What do you feel is the most important duty of the Register of Deeds?
The duty to the public to answer questions and assist, be available to the public, on-line security, fight against robo signing, we must reject robo signed papers. The duty to the public is important.

What is the number one issue you would address once elected?
I plan to fight fraud, follow Indexing&Standards and continue to be the best run Registry of Deeds with excellent customer service.
