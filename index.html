Who is Joe?

Joseph Gentleman and his wife Jeannine Gentleman (Langlais) both natives of Lynn Massachusetts and graduates of Lynn Classical High School have lived in Peabody for over the past 28 years. Joseph also has a late son Tim Gentleman and late Daughter in-law Laura Gentleman with five grandchildren. Joseph worked for over twenty years at the Registry of Deeds in Salem under John O'Brien the longtime registrar running one of the best run registries in Massachusetts. Joseph served his country as a U.S. Navy Veteran, former Secretary-Treasurer for the IAM Local Union S-25 Ship Builders in Boston, held many leadership roles in the community including Founder of the Essex County Regional Lions Club in 2015, current president of the Lynn Lions Club, past president of the Visual Aids Foundation Inc., past vice president of the Eye Mobile Inc. and past Director of the Sight and Hearing Foundation Inc. and is a member of the Lions Clubs International organization which donates humanitarian service around the globe.

Background and Experience

Joe and his wife Jeannine, both Lynn natives, have lived in Peabody for over 28 years. They have five grandchildren. Joe worked for over twenty years at the Registry of Deeds in Salem, under John O'Brien.

He served as a U.S. Navy Veteran, former Secretary-Treasurer for IAM Local Union S-25 Ship Builders in Boston, and held various leadership roles in community organizations.

Joe founded the Essex County Regional Lions Club in 2015 and has been actively involved in numerous community service projects.

Campaign Goals

Joe is running to protect home ownership, fight fraud, and enforce the Indexing and Formatting Standards in Massachusetts. He aims to enhance customer service, promote the Homestead Act, and improve registry services to make them more accessible and efficient for the community.

Platform and Community Commitment

Joe believes in enhancing customer service and making e-filing more accessible. His goal is to bring his experience and dedication to the role, ensuring the registry serves the public efficiently and effectively.

Lions Club Leadership

As President of the Lynn Lions Club, Joe has demonstrated exceptional leadership, organizing community service projects and events that have positively impacted many lives. His role has strengthened his management skills, making him well-equipped to serve as Registrar of Deeds.

Joe's involvement as Director at Camp Lions showcases his dedication to community service and his ability to lead and manage large-scale initiatives. These experiences translate directly to the organizational skills needed for the role of Registrar of Deeds.
